<p><p>Therapeutic enzyme drugs are classified into three main market types based on their administration routes: injectable, oral, and topical. Injectable enzymes are administered via syringes or infusions, offering rapid absorption and immediate therapeutic effects, often used in critical conditions. Oral enzymes are taken in pill or liquid form, providing convenience but may have variable absorption rates. Topical enzymes are applied directly to the skin for localized treatment, often used for wound healing or skin conditions, allowing targeted action with minimal systemic effects.</p></p>

<p><p>The laboratory chillers market primarily includes two types: water-cooled and air-cooled chillers. Water-cooled laboratory chillers utilize water as a cooling medium, offering high efficiency and consistent temperature control, making them ideal for large-scale operations. In contrast, air-cooled laboratory chillers use air to dissipate heat, providing a more compact and cost-effective solution for smaller facilities or where water access is limited. Both types serve critical roles in maintaining optimal conditions for sensitive laboratory processes and equipment.</p></p>
